Campaign Strategy & Ideation

One University.
Two Distinct Voices.

The challenge: How do you market a university that locals see as a "commuter school" but the rest of the country sees as an "island paradise"?

My solution was to split the signal.

The Insight

During initial discovery, I realized we were suffering from an identity crisis. We were trying to use one generic message—"Join the Island"—for everyone.

The problem? Locals didn't care about the beach; they live there. They cared about cost and career outcomes. Meanwhile, out-of-state students didn't know we existed, but were looking for a unique adventure.

I pitched a dual-campaign architecture running simultaneously:

VS

Audience A: Locals

"Why leave home when you have a tier-one university in your backyard?"

Audience B: National

"Study where others vacation. The only university on its own island."

The Primary Tagline

STAY CLOSE.
GO FAR.

Hometown Advantage

I pivoted the messaging to focus on pragmatic value. For the local market, the "Island" isn't a novelty—it's home. We needed to sell the outcome, not just the location.

"Strategic Intent: Equating local proximity with global opportunity."

The Primary Tagline

THE BEACH IS
THE BEGINNING.

Island Life Pull

For out-of-state students, we sold a lifestyle upgrade. We positioned the university as a destination that offers a Tier-One education in a vacation setting.

"Strategic Intent: Leading with the 'Island' USP to open doors to academic discussion."

The Combined Impact

Local Enrollment

2x

Out-of-State Leads

Attributed directly to the "Island Life" social campaign.
