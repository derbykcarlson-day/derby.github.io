Design Rationale

The Strategic Shift

How data-driven design turned a routine notice into a high-conversion invitation.

👁️
01

Visual Hierarchy

The original email was a "wall of text." I implemented a card-based layout and hero section, allowing prospective students to identify the Date, Time, and CTA in under 3 seconds.

📣
02

The "Student" Tone

We pivoted from "Institutional Passive" to "Relational Active." By shifting the narrative from what the office is doing to what the student will experience, we built immediate affinity.

🎯
03

Friction Removal

The primary RSVP goal was isolated. I utilized a high-contrast button with generous padding and shadow depth, optimizing the mobile "thumb-zone" for easier conversions.

The Real Story

We Had to Stop Sending.

The most telling sign of success wasn't a metric — it was a problem we'd never had before.

🚫

Capacity Reached · One Week Early

We had to pull the final email campaign before it sent.

Typically, the week leading up to the event is peak sign-up time — the final reminder emails are what drive the last push of registrations. But because of the redesign's impact, we hit our event capacity 6 days before that window even opened. The last email was ready to go. We never sent it.

→ How It Unfolded

Dec 1 — Feb 10

Slow Baseline · Original Emails

Registrations trickled in at an average of 1.8 per day using the original, text-heavy email template. No urgency, no visual hook.

Feb 11

Redesign Launches

The redesigned email dropped with a new visual hierarchy, student-centric tone, and isolated CTA. That single day saw 25 registrations — a 10× spike from the daily average.

Feb 12 – Feb 22

Sustained Momentum

Following the launch, registrations maintained a significantly higher floor, peaking with 37 sign-ups in early March following the second email.

~March 23

Event Cap Hit · Final Email Pulled

The event reached full capacity a full 6 days before the event date. The final reminder email was never sent. There was simply no one left to invite.
