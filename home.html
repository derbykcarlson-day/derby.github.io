Core Methodology

The Philosophy

01

Relational First

In the modern day, emails have become oversaturated with junk. The most natural way to overcome that is too focus on building real connections first, so that when we eventually ask them to apply, it feels like an invitation.

02

"So What?"

I’m a stickler for value. Before we hit send, I ask: "Why should a student care about this?" Every email and SMS needs to answer that question. If we aren't providing immediate value or solving a problem, we shouldn't be taking up space in their inbox.

03

Frictionless Flow

Good CRM architecture should stay invisible. My job is to find the hurdles—the confusing forms, the broken links, the vague instructions, people complaining in cross-departmental meetings—and clear them away so the path to enrollment is completely seamless.

04

Scalable Quality

Automating the "boring stuff" isn't about cutting corners. It's about freeing up people. I build the systems that handle the heavy lifting, allowing for enrollment teams to focus on the high-touch, meaningful interactions that actually move a student to joining our communities.
